# Contributor Licence Agreements

Meshum is distributed under the [GNU AGPL-3.0](../../LICENSE.md), dual-licensed with a
commercial option. To let Us offer the Work under both the AGPL-3.0 **and** separate
commercial or proprietary licences, every contributor must grant the relicensing rights
described below. This is why We ask you to sign a Contributor Licence Agreement ("CLA")
before your first contribution can be merged.

These documents are **not legal advice**. They are adapted from the Apache CLA 2.0 and
Project Harmony. If you have questions, contact **legal@meshum.dev**.

## Which agreement do I sign?

| Your situation | Agreement |
| --- | --- |
| You are contributing as an **individual**, on your own behalf, and your employer has **no** claim to the work you submit | [Individual CLA (ICLA)](icla.md) |
| Your employer (or any entity that controls your IP) **does** have rights to your contributions, or you are contributing on behalf of a company | [Corporate CLA (CCLA)](ccla.md), signed by an authorised representative |

If you are unsure whether your employer has rights to your contributions, ask them — and
see Section 4 of the [ICLA](icla.md). When in doubt, sign the ICLA **and** have your
employer sign the CCLA.

## What am I granting?

In short (the agreements themselves are authoritative):

- A **perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free, irrevocable** copyright licence
  in your contributions, **including the right for Us to sublicense and relicense** them
  under any terms We choose — most importantly the AGPL-3.0 **and** commercial licences.
- A parallel **patent licence** for any patent claims your contribution necessarily
  infringes.

You **keep** your copyright. Nothing here assigns it to Us. You may still use your own
contributions for any other purpose.

## How do I sign?

Signing is automated through GitHub for pull requests. The first time you open a pull
request, the CLA bot will leave a comment asking you to sign. Copy and paste the exact
phrase from the comment — it references the [ICLA](icla.md) — as a new comment on the pull
request. The `CLA` status check will flip green once you have signed; it is a **required**
check, so the pull request cannot be merged until then.

Corporate contributors should additionally have an authorised representative sign the
[CCLA](ccla.md). Opening a linked issue from the pull request is the easiest way to start
that conversation — mention it and a maintainer will guide you through it.
